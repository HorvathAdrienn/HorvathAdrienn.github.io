Everyone wants to start somewhere. But where should one start? How do you build your portfolio from scratch? Stay with me and find it out!

1. Inspire yourself!

Browse the internet and do a research. Try to analyze what others have done on their website, how did they do it, and make a note out of it. If you have some experience in webdevelopment, that's an advantage. If not, don't worry! I'll lend a guide in the future. Until keep up and search! I'm sure you'ill find your way and decide what language to use, which's our next point

2. Choose the appropiate languages that suit you!

Are you an HTML&CSS only person or do you like to use PHP too? Do you prefer static or responsive webdesign? How much would you use animations?

“A successful website does three things: It attracts the right kinds of visitors. Guides them to the main services or product you offer. Collect Contact details for future ongoing relation.”

Mohamed Saad Source Title

I put a quote above that explains my point. Do what you think is right, but keep in might that it might not work right away. In my opinion you can be creative as sky's the limit, but you should not forget, that carrying things too far will lead towards a very messy, chaotic website. Decide what language will you use according to what do you want to achieve. If your plan changes, and want to use a third or fourth one, so be it!

3. Does look matter that much?

If your navigation is straightforward, and you don't put a video in the body section as background, your website will be more appealing and it will also load faster in the browser. So yes, it does matter in my view and if you have decided what language to use, that's where the fun part starts! Try out different methods! How your navigation looks like on the left, or right? What about a hamburger menu? It might make your navigation's setup more practical, as on android hamburger menu works perfectly! It also matters what colors do you choose. For example a white text on a red button can be both great and horrible, depending on red's strength. If too bright, the text will be blurry and hard-to-read. So at this case a darker color makes the text pop out and appealing to the eyes as well.
